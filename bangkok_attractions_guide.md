# Bangkok Attractions & Kids Activities Near Chatrium Grand Bangkok
## Charoen Krung Road, Sathorn/Riverside Area

---

## COOL PLACES TO SEE (8 Places)

### 1. Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (~58,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~5 km (20-30 min by taxi, or ferry across the river)
- **Description:** Iconic riverside temple with a towering spire decorated with fragments of Chinese porcelain and ceramics. At night, Wat Arun glows spectacularly. One of Bangkok's most photographed landmarks.
- **Opening hours:** 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM daily
- **Entrance fee:** 100 THB (~14 AED)
- **Kid-friendly:** Yes - climbing the steep steps is an adventure for older kids

### 2. Wat Pho (Temple of the Reclining Buddha)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (~40,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~5 km (20-30 min by taxi)
- **Description:** Home to the massive 46-meter gilded Reclining Buddha. Features 108 bronze bowls where visitors drop coins for good luck. Also the birthplace of traditional Thai massage.
- **Opening hours:** 8:30 AM - 6:30 PM daily
- **Entrance fee:** 300 THB (~42 AED)
- **Kid-friendly:** Yes - kids love the giant Buddha and coin-dropping ritual

### 3. ICONSIAM & SookSiam
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (ICONSIAM) | 4.8/5 (SookSiam)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~4 km (riverside, accessible by free shuttle boat)
- **Description:** Mega riverside lifestyle complex with 7,000+ shops across 10 stories. SookSiam on the ground floor is a themed indoor floating market showcasing treasures from all 77 Thai provinces - rare regional street food, handicrafts, and cultural performances. The 7th-floor Napalai Terrace offers free panoramic river views.
- **Opening hours:** 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM daily
- **Entrance fee:** Free
- **Kid-friendly:** Yes - has a Kid's Cinema with play area, slides, and ball pit

### 4. King Power Mahanakhon SkyWalk
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.6/5 (~362 reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~3 km (BTS Chong Nonsi station)
- **Description:** Thailand's highest observation deck at 314 meters on the 78th floor of Bangkok's tallest building. Features a terrifying glass-floor tray where you walk over the city, plus 360-degree panoramic views. Indoor and outdoor observation areas.
- **Opening hours:** 10:00 AM - 12:00 AM daily (last admission 11:00 PM)
- **Entrance fee:** 880-1,050 THB adults (~123-147 AED) | 250 THB children (~35 AED)
- **Kid-friendly:** Yes for older kids (glass tray is thrilling); shoe covers required

### 5. Talad Noi (Street Art Neighborhood)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.0/5
- **Distance from hotel:** ~3.5 km (walkable from the hotel area along Charoen Krung)
- **Description:** Bangkok's oldest neighborhood and its coolest street art district. Hidden murals in narrow alleys, the 200-year-old Chow Sue Kong Shrine, Holy Rosary Church (1787), and old engine workshops coexisting with world-class graffiti. Excellent cafes like Mother Roaster and Songwat Coffee Roaster.
- **Opening hours:** Open area - visit anytime (best during daylight)
- **Entrance fee:** Free
- **Kid-friendly:** Yes - the street art treasure hunt appeals to all ages

### 6. Asiatique The Riverfront
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.0/5 (~20,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~4 km (free shuttle boat from Sathorn/BTS Saphan Taksin pier)
- **Description:** Massive open-air night market and entertainment complex in former East Asiatic Company docks along the river. Features 1,500+ boutiques, restaurants, a giant Ferris wheel (Asiatique Sky), Calypso Cabaret shows, and Muay Thai Live performances. Best visited at sunset and evening.
- **Opening hours:** 4:00 PM - 12:00 AM daily
- **Entrance fee:** Free (Ferris wheel ~400 THB / ~56 AED)
- **Kid-friendly:** Yes - Ferris wheel, carnival games, street performers

### 7. Jim Thompson House Museum
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (~15,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~4 km (BTS National Stadium station)
- **Description:** Beautifully preserved traditional Thai teak house museum, former home of the American "Thai Silk King" who mysteriously disappeared in 1967. Magnificent collection of Southeast Asian art set in a lush tropical garden. Guided tours only, available in 5 languages.
- **Opening hours:** 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily (last tour at 5:00 PM)
- **Entrance fee:** 250 THB adults (~35 AED) | 150 THB ages 10-21 (~21 AED) | Free under 10
- **Kid-friendly:** Moderate - the mystery story engages older kids

### 8. Lumphini Park
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (~10,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~2.5 km (BTS Sala Daeng / MRT Silom)
- **Description:** Bangkok's "Central Park" - a 142-acre green oasis in the heart of the city. Paddle boats on the lake, playgrounds, library, jogging paths, and wild monitor lizards (up to 2 meters long!) roaming freely. Turtles, birds, and fish throughout.
- **Opening hours:** 4:30 AM - 9:00 PM daily
- **Entrance fee:** Free (paddle boats ~40 THB / ~6 AED per 30 min)
- **Kid-friendly:** Absolutely - kids love spotting the giant monitor lizards

## KIDS ACTIVITIES (6 Options)

### 1. SEA LIFE Bangkok Ocean World
- **Age suitability:** All ages (toddlers to teens)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (~10,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~4.5 km (BTS Siam, inside Siam Paragon basement B1-B2)
- **Description:** Southeast Asia's largest aquarium - 110,000 sq ft with 30,000 marine animals across 400+ species. Walk through underwater tunnels, ride a glass-bottom boat over sharks, touch starfish at the Interactive Rockpool, and visit the Penguin Ice Playground. Plan ~2 hours.
- **Price:** ~990-1,600 THB adults (~139-224 AED) | discounts available on Klook/Traveloka
- **Opening hours:** 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM daily (last entry 7:00 PM)
- **Highlight:** Shark Walk - walk on glass above 5 species of sharks

### 2. Safari World Bangkok
- **Age suitability:** Ages 3+ (all ages enjoy it)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.5/5 (~8,000+ reviews)
- **Distance from hotel:** ~40 km (1 hour drive, northern Bangkok outskirts)
- **Description:** Two parks in one: Safari Park (drive-through with giraffes, zebras, lions, tigers roaming free) and Marine Park (zoo + live shows - dolphins, sea lions, birds, action stunts). Arrive early to catch all shows. Feeding experiences available (giraffes, birds).
- **Price:** Weekdays 550 THB (~77 AED) | Weekends 880 THB (~123 AED) | Kids/Seniors 250 THB (~35 AED) | Safari coach 100 THB extra
- **Opening hours:** Safari Park 9:00 AM - 4:30 PM | Marine Park 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
- **Highlight:** Drive-through safari with free-roaming African animals

### 3. KidZania Bangkok
- **Age suitability:** Ages 4-14 (ideal 5-12)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.0/5
- **Distance from hotel:** ~4.5 km (5th floor, Siam Paragon, BTS Siam)
- **Description:** A mini city where kids role-play 80+ real-world jobs - pilot, firefighter, chef, doctor, TV presenter, and more. Kids earn "kidZos" currency and learn about careers through play. Extremely engaging - kids often don't want to leave. Plan 3-4 hours.
- **Price:** Kids weekday 1,100 THB (~154 AED) | weekend 1,350 THB (~189 AED) | Adults weekday 640 THB (~90 AED) | weekend 800 THB (~112 AED)
- **Opening hours:** 11:00 AM - 5:00 PM daily
- **Highlight:** Kids fly a plane simulator and perform surgery in a hospital

### 4. Chao Phraya River Hop-On Hop-Off Tourist Boat
- **Age suitability:** All ages (stroller/wheelchair accessible)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.0/5
- **Distance from hotel:** Departs from Sathorn Pier (~3 km, BTS Saphan Taksin)
- **Description:** Air-conditioned tourist boats with unlimited hop-on hop-off rides all day. Stops at Grand Palace, Wat Arun, Chinatown, Khao San Road, and more. Commentary onboard. Life jackets at every seat, onboard toilets. Boats every 15-30 minutes.
- **Price:** ~150-200 THB adults (~21-28 AED) | Free for children under 90 cm
- **Operating hours:** 8:30 AM - 7:15 PM (varies by direction)
- **Highlight:** Best way to see riverside Bangkok with minimal walking

### 5. Pororo AquaPark
- **Age suitability:** Ages 2-12 (toddler pools to big slides)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.0/5
- **Distance from hotel:** ~20 km (CentralPlaza Bangna, near Si Iam monorail)
- **Description:** Thailand's only themed rooftop water park, based on the Korean animated character Pororo the Little Penguin. 10,000 sqm with 9 pools, water slides ranging from gentle to thrilling, lazy river, and splash zones. Both shallow toddler areas and deeper pools for older kids.
- **Price:** ~280-350 THB (~39-49 AED) per person
- **Operating hours:** Weekdays 10:30 AM - 7:00 PM | Weekends 10:00 AM - 7:00 PM
- **Highlight:** Rooftop setting with Pororo character meet-and-greets

### 6. Bangkok Snake Farm (Queen Saovabha Memorial Institute)
- **Age suitability:** Ages 5+ (educational, not a petting zoo)
- **TripAdvisor Rating:** 4.0/5
- **Distance from hotel:** ~3 km (800m from BTS Sala Daeng / MRT Silom)
- **Description:** Run by the Thai Red Cross, this unique attraction specializes in venomous snakes. Educational live shows demonstrate venom extraction. Kids learn about different snake species, anti-venom production, and Thai wildlife. Genuinely fascinating and unusual.
- **Price:** 250 THB adults (~35 AED) | 50 THB children (~7 AED)
- **Operating hours:** Weekdays 9:30 AM - 3:30 PM | Weekends 9:30 AM - 1:00 PM
- **Highlight:** Live venom extraction demonstration - unforgettable for kids

## PRACTICAL TIPS

- **Getting around:** BTS Skytrain + Chao Phraya boats are the best combo. Avoid taxis during rush hour (7-9 AM, 5-8 PM).
- **Sathorn Pier (BTS Saphan Taksin)** is your gateway to all riverside attractions - ferries to Wat Arun, Asiatique, ICONSIAM.
- **Best combo day:** Morning temples (Wat Pho + Wat Arun by ferry) -> Lunch at ICONSIAM SookSiam -> Afternoon SEA LIFE or KidZania at Siam Paragon -> Evening at Asiatique.
- **Currency note:** 1 AED = approximately 7.1 THB (March 2026).
- **Weather:** March in Bangkok is hot (32-36°C). Do outdoor activities morning/evening; malls and indoor attractions midday.
