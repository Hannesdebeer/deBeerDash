Can You Buy Property in Thailand?
Short answer: Yes — with important rules
The Good News

Foreigners can own a condominium unit 100% freehold in their own name in Thailand. This is permanent, inheritable, and transferable ownership with a full title deed. No company needed, no nominee — it's yours outright.

The 49% Foreign Quota Rule Key Rule

Under the Thai Condominium Act, foreigners can collectively own up to 49% of the total floor area in any registered condominium building. The other 51% must be Thai-owned. Always get written confirmation from the condo's Juristic Person that foreign quota is available before paying anything.

Never Use a Nominee Company

Using a Thai company with fake Thai shareholders to buy land is illegal. Penalties: up to 3 years prison and THB 1,000,000 fines. Thai authorities have been actively investigating and prosecuting these in 2024–2025. The foreign "owner" has zero legal protection. Just buy a freehold condo.

How to Buy — Step by Step
The legal process for a foreigner buying a freehold condo
1
Find a property — Use agents, listing sites, or visit in person. Shortlist 3–5 options.
2
Verify foreign quota — Get written confirmation from the condo Juristic Person that the 49% foreign quota has space. No confirmation = walk away.
3
Hire YOUR OWN lawyer — An independent, bilingual (English/Thai) property lawyer. Never use the developer's or agent's lawyer. Budget THB 30,000–100,000.
4
Due diligence — Title search at Land Office (verify no encumbrances, mortgages, liens). Check building permits, EIA compliance, developer financials.
5
Reservation & deposit — Sign reservation agreement. Pay booking fee (THB 50,000–200,000, usually non-refundable). Then sign Sale & Purchase Agreement and pay 10–30% deposit.
6
Transfer funds from SA — Wire purchase price in foreign currency (ZAR/USD) from your SA bank to your Thai bank account. The Thai bank converts to THB and issues the FET Form. This is critical — no FET = no ownership registration.
7
Transfer at Land Office — Both parties attend (or via Power of Attorney). Title deed transferred, taxes & fees paid, ownership officially registered.
The FET Form Is Non-Negotiable

Your funds must come from outside Thailand in foreign currency (not THB). The bank memo must state: "For purchase of condominium unit [number] at [name] for [Your Full Name]." The name must match your passport and SPA exactly. No FET = no freehold registration at the Land Office.

Title Deed Types
TitleSecurityNotes
Chanote (Nor Sor 4 Jor)HighestFull freehold, GPS-surveyed. The gold standard.
Nor Sor 3 GorHighOfficially surveyed, strong rights.
Nor Sor 3MediumNot precisely surveyed. 30-day public notice for transfers.
Sor Kor 1LowCannot be sold or mortgaged. Avoid.

Rule: Only buy with a Chanote title. Anything less — walk away.

Common Scams

1. Foreign quota fraud — Sold a unit when the 49% is already full
2. Nominee company schemes — "Perfectly legal" (they're not)
3. Guaranteed 8–10% rental returns — Often inflated into the purchase price
4. Phantom off-plan projects — Take deposits, never build
5. Missing FET form — Pay cash locally = cannot register ownership
6. Dual pricing — Foreigners quoted much higher than Thais

FIA Process

1. Log into SARS eFiling
2. Navigate to "Tax Compliance Status" → "Foreign Investment Allowance"
3. Complete details about source of funds and purpose
4. Upload: income statements, bank statements, property purchase agreement
5. SARS issues a TCS PIN (7–14 business days)
6. Give the TCS PIN to your bank/forex dealer to process the transfer
7. Valid for 12 months

Eligibility

You must be: SA tax resident, over 18, fully tax compliant (all returns filed, no outstanding debt). Property purchases are specifically listed as an approved use of the FIA.

Tax Implications
SA Taxes on Thai Property

As a SA tax resident, you're taxed on worldwide income:

EventSA Tax Treatment
Rental incomeDeclared as foreign income on ITR12. Taxed at your marginal rate. Claim Thai tax paid as credit.
Capital gain on sale40% inclusion rate (40% of gain added to taxable income). Annual R40,000 exclusion. Claim Thai WHT as credit.
Thai taxes paidForeign tax credit under the SA-Thailand DTA (in force since 1 Jan 1997). Prevents double taxation.
Double Tax Agreement (DTA) In Force Since 1997

Thailand can tax your rental income and capital gains (property is in Thailand). South Africa also taxes it (worldwide income), but must credit Thai taxes paid. You won't be double-taxed, but you must declare everything to SARS.

Don't Forget SARS

Declare all Thai property income and gains annually. From ~2029, SA will participate in automatic exchange of foreign property data — SARS will know. Non-disclosure penalties are severe.

Visas & Staying in Thailand
Owning property does NOT give you a visa — here are your options
Important

Buying a condo does not automatically grant any visa or right to stay in Thailand. You still need a valid visa. But there are several good options.

THB 3M Property Visa New Oct 2025
1-year renewable

Buy a completed freehold condo worth THB 3M+ and qualify for a renewable 1-year stay. Must be registered after Oct 1, 2020. Off-plan does NOT qualify — must be completed and transferred.

Thailand Privilege (Elite) Visa Premium
5–20 yearsTHB 650,000 – 5,000,000

Membership-based long-term visa. Airport VIP, lounge access, annual health check. No income or age requirement — just the fee. Best option for regular visitors.

Long-Term Resident (LTR) Visa
10 years renewable

Wealthy Global Citizen: USD 1M+ assets + USD 500K in Thai investments.
Wealthy Pensioner: Age 50+, USD 80K/year income or USD 40–80K + USD 250K Thai investment.
Includes 17% flat income tax rate and work permit.

Practical Tips
What to know about owning property in a tropical island
Tropical Climate Challenges

Humidity & mould — The biggest issue. Run AC or dehumidifiers regularly, even when absent. Mould can destroy furniture and walls in weeks.

Salt air corrosion — Metal fixtures, electronics, and AC units degrade faster near the coast.

Pest control — Regular treatment needed (termites, ants, mosquitoes).

AC servicing — Clean filters monthly; professional service every 3–6 months.

Storm season — Oct–Dec brings heavy rain. Ensure adequate insurance.

If absent — Hire management or have someone check monthly at minimum.

Top Tips for SA Buyers

• Get your TCS PIN from SARS before you start shopping — it takes 7–14 days
• Open a Thai bank account in person (bring passport, proof of address, Thai phone number)
• Wire money in ZAR or USD — never send THB
• Visit in person before buying — photos can be misleading
• Check the foreign quota in writing before paying any deposit
• Budget 3–6% above purchase price for fees
• Use Wise or OFX for better forex rates vs your bank
• Factor in both SA and Thai tax when calculating returns
• If buying for rental: Bophut or Chaweng for highest occupancy
• If buying for lifestyle: Maenam or Lamai for value & peace

Iran War Impact on Dubai Property (Feb–Mar 2026)
What Happened

On 28 Feb 2026, US and Israel launched airstrikes on Iran. Iran retaliated with missiles and drones hitting Dubai directly — Burj Al Arab, Fairmont The Palm, DXB Airport, and Jebel Ali Port were all struck. Over 1,800 projectiles have been fired at the UAE since.

Stock MarketCrashed

DFM Real Estate Index dropped 30% in 2 weeks, wiping out all 2026 gains. DFM closed for 2 sessions. Real estate bonds saw steep losses.

Actual Property PricesHolding

Transaction prices have not crashed. 3,570 deals worth AED 11.93B completed in the first week. Viewings recovered 75% after initial shock. 60% of purchases are cash.

Key TakeawaySentiment Shock

This is a sentiment shock, not a structural collapse — so far. The stock index crashed but actual property prices are holding. However, this is the first time Dubai itself has been physically struck, which is qualitatively different from previous regional conflicts where Dubai served as the safe haven beneficiary.

The Verdict

It’s a nuanced moment. Dubai has converted from a one-way bull market into a market that finally prices risk again. If you have a long-term horizon (5+ years) and can stomach short-term volatility, the distress deals (20–35% below market) currently available may represent a rare buying window — especially for villas where supply is severely constrained. If you need certainty or plan to flip short-term, wait for the conflict to resolve. Prioritise completed, freehold, ready-to-transfer assets in prime communities with proven demand (Dubai Hills, Arabian Ranches, Town Square, Mudon).
